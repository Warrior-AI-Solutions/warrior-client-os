## 4. Multi-Agent Business Architecture

### Agent Roster (Named Agents from Conversations)

| Agent | Level | Function |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Breakdown Coach** | Level 1 | Daily check-in: "did you do the work?" Accountability pulse |
| **Breakthrough Coach** | Level 2 | Quality of work: "was the work good?" Depth assessment |
| **Coordinating Agent** | Orchestrator | Manages all other agents, routes tasks, maintains context |
| **Onboarding Agent** | First 3 weeks | Small steps daily, reduces cognitive overload during adoption |
| **Product Agent** | Strategic | Generates product/niche/market ideas from conversations and context |
| **Sales Agent** | Revenue | Automates calls, texts, email outreach — fills calendar with qualified leads |

### How the System Works Together
The Coordinating Agent sits at the top of the stack. It ingests context from all interactions (coaching sessions, check-ins, CRM data) and routes tasks to specialist agents. The result is a business operating system that runs 24/7 without human oversight gaps.

### The Coaching Loop (Core IP)
```
Breakdown Coach (Level 1 — daily)
    ↓ [confirms work happened]
Breakthrough Coach (Level 2 — quality gate)
    ↓ [confirms work was good]
Coordinating Agent (orchestrates next action)
    ↓ [routes to Product / Sales / Calendar agent]
```

This loop replaces the traditional quarterly check-in with real-time accountability infrastructure.
